Case Study: BMW Car Sales Analysis (2019–2023)

Analyzing BMW car sales data from 2019–2023 to uncover growth drivers, regional opportunities, and customer trends. Revenue grew from $0.90bn to $1.13bn in 2023 — a +24.7% increase.

Project Overview

This project explored BMW’s global car sales performance, focusing on revenue growth, model-level demand, and market opportunities. We transformed raw sales data into actionable insights through Power BI dashboards, data storytelling, and KPI tracking.

In 2023, BMW achieved $1.13bn in revenue, compared to $0.90bn in 2022 — a +24.7% year-on-year growth. Flagship models like the BMW Z4, 8 Series, and M4 drove the surge, supported by stronger demand in North America and Latin America.

The Challenge

Despite strong demand, BMW faced several strategic challenges:

  • Monitoring revenue growth across multiple years and regions.
  • Identifying which models and markets were driving the most impact.
  • Aligning sales channels (wholesale, dealerships, online) to customer preferences.
  • Communicating insights visually to executives instead of through raw spreadsheets.
  • Spotting growth opportunities in the premium & electric segments.

The Approach & Solution

Our solution blended data engineering, analytics, and visual storytelling:

  • Data Preparation → Cleaned raw data, standardized revenue values, and validated channels.
  • Exploratory Analysis → Compared sales across models, countries, and time periods.
  • Visualization → Designed an executive Power BI dashboard with revenue growth KPIs.
  • Business Insights → Highlighted premium model performance, emerging EV growth, and regional shifts.

The analysis revealed:

  • Electric & hybrid sales are rapidly expanding in urban regions.
  • Returning customers are spending more on premium BMW models.
  • South Africa showed exceptional growth (+67.3%), a major expansion opportunity.
  • Online sales continue to grow, signaling a shift in customer buying behavior.

By aligning inventory and marketing with these insights, BMW is better positioned to accelerate growth, optimize supply chains, and capture demand in high-growth markets.
