Case Study: BMW Car Sales Analysis (2019–2023)

Analyzing BMW car sales data from 2019–2023 to uncover growth drivers, regional opportunities, and customer trends. Revenue grew from $0.90bn to $1.13bn in 2023 — a +24.7% increase.

Project Overview

This project explored BMW’s global car sales performance, focusing on revenue growth, model trends, and regional insights. By leveraging dashboards and analytics, raw sales data was transformed into actionable insights for executives and dealerships.

In 2023, BMW achieved $1.13bn in revenue, compared to $0.90bn in the prior year — a +24.7% growth. Top-performing models like the BMW Z4, 8 Series, and M4 were key contributors, with demand strongest in United States, Mexico, and Colombia.

The Challenge

Despite strong demand, BMW faced several challenges:

  • Tracking year-on-year revenue and sales growth effectively.
  • Identifying which countries and models were driving performance shifts.
  • Presenting insights in executive-friendly dashboards instead of spreadsheets.
  • Understanding channel effectiveness across wholesale, dealership, and online.

The Approach & Solution

To address these challenges, we combined data cleaning, exploratory analysis, and interactive dashboards:

  • Data Preparation → Fixed missing values, standardized revenue formats, validated sales channels.
  • Exploratory Analysis → Compared sales trends across models, regions, and channels.
  • Visualization → Built Power BI dashboards for clear executive reporting.
  • Insights → Translated raw data into growth strategies.

Insights highlighted growth opportunities in premium and electric models, loyalty programs for returning customers, and scaling sales in high-growth regions such as South Africa (+67.3%). This analysis positioned BMW to align inventory, marketing, and pricing strategies with evolving demand.
