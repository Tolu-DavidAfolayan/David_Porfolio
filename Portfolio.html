Titanic Machine Learning- Survival Prediction

The Titanic survival prediction model uses machine learning to forecast passenger outcomes based on age, gender, class, and more. It uncovers survival bias in socioeconomic status and gender roles, reflecting historical inequalities. Feature engineering enhances model accuracy by interpreting family size, titles, and fare brackets. Classification algorithms like Random Forest and Logistic Regression were tested for performance. The model helps demonstrate how data science can extract patterns from tragedy. This project is a foundational case in predictive modeling and real-world application of ML ethics...

Telco Customer Retention

The Telco Customer Retention report delivers critical insights into why telecom customers churn and which user segments are most at risk. Built entirely in Power BI without any code, it empowers decision-makers with an intuitive dashboard to visualize churn patterns, identify high-risk profiles, and evaluate the revenue impact of customer loss. Segment-level analysis reveals that customers on monthly contracts and those using manual payment methods are more likely to leave.
